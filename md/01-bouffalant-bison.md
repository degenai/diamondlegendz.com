# Case 01 — Bouffalant / American bison → Earth Ox

**Question:** Does Bouffalant (American bison-coded) belong in the Ox row, or does it orphan via the Tier 3 "non-Chinese fauna" rule?

## Design lore

Per [Bulbapedia: Bouffalant](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bouffalant_(Pok%C3%A9mon)), Bouffalant is "based on a North American bison (*Bison bison*) and an afro hairstyle." Pure Normal type, classified as the "Bash Buffalo Pokémon," signature move Head Charge.

Biologically: American bison (*Bison bison*) and water buffalo (*Bubalus bubalis*) are both in family Bovidae, subfamily Bovinae, but different genera. American bison was unknown to pre-modern Chinese culture; water buffalo has been Chinese rice-paddy plow infrastructure since at least Han dynasty.

## Chinese language reading

牛 in modern Mandarin covers all bovines: cattle (黄牛), water buffalo (水牛), yak (牦牛), wild ox (野牛). The standard term for American bison is **北美野牛** ("North American wild ox") — explicitly subsumed under 牛 by character semantics. Bouffalant therefore passes the Chinese-character test cleanly; the orphan reasoning has to come from somewhere else.

## Strict-rule verdict (pre-market-test)

The earlier "non-Chinese fauna" Tier 3 rule orphaned Bouffalant because American bison wasn't part of pre-Han Chinese cultural memory. This is a defensible *historicist* position but it relies on freezing the zodiac canon at one moment in time. The same rule didn't cleanly orphan water buffalo (Asian, fine) and didn't cleanly include yak (Tibetan, contiguous Chinese sphere) — the rule was doing fragile work.

## Market evidence

**Pokémon Sword/Shield Year of the Ox 2021 Max Raid event** ran a themed encounter table that explicitly featured **Tauros, Miltank, AND Bouffalant** as the three ox-coded Pokémon in rotation. This is The Pokémon Company empirically declaring Bouffalant an Ox-row Pokémon for Year of the Ox merchandising purposes. Source: [GameSpot — Pokémon Sword/Shield Lunar New Year Event](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/pokemon-sword-and-shield-lunar-new-year-event-now-live-features-shiny-tauros/1100-6486629/) and [NintendoSoup — "Year of the Ox" Themed Max Raid](https://nintendosoup.com/year-of-the-ox-themed-max-raid-event-now-live-for-pokemon-sword-shield/).

The Pokémon GO Lunar New Year 2021 event likewise featured Tauros and Miltank as the bovine highlights, with Bouffalant available via increased spawns in some regions. Source: [Pokémon GO Hub — Lunar New Year 2021](https://pokemongohub.net/post/event/lunar-new-year-2021-event/).

## Element placement

Bouffalant is pure Normal type. Per R5, Normal-as-mundane → Earth. Earth Ox is the natural cell. Tauros (also Normal) and Miltank (also Normal) are already there under the same R5 extension. Adding Bouffalant joins the Normal-as-mundane bovine cluster cleanly.

## Recommendation

**Restore Bouffalant to Earth Ox.** TPC has empirically ratified the placement; modern Mandarin includes bison under 野牛; the element fits via the existing R5 extension. The "non-Chinese fauna" rule is too fragile to override TPC's own market behavior here. Keep the "non-Chinese fauna" rule active for cases where (a) TPC has *not* zodiac-merchandised the animal AND (b) Chinese language doesn't include it under a Wuxing-relevant character.

**Status:** Restored. Earth Ox cluster is now Terrakion + Tauros + Bouffalant + Miltank (depth 4).
