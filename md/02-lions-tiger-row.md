# Case 02 — Lions in the Tiger row

**Question:** Should Pyroar, Solgaleo, Entei, Suicune, Hisuian Arcanine (lion or lion-tiger coded) be restored to the Tiger row under the market-acceptance test?

## The structural problem

虎 (hǔ) in Chinese means tiger specifically. Chinese has separate characters for lion (狮 shī), leopard (豹 bào), and house cat (猫 māo). The character does *not* broaden the way 鼠 (any rodent) and 羊 (goat/sheep/ram) do. A culturally educated Chinese reader looking at Pyroar would call it 狮, not 虎.

Lions are also Buddhist-imported iconography in China — guardian-lion statues (the "foo dogs" of Western tourism), lion dance — arrived from Indian/Central Asian Buddhism, post-Han. Chinese culture distinguishes 虎 (native fauna, mythologized as the Western Tiger of the four guardians) from 狮 (imported symbol).

Per the strict China-max ruleset (R6 + criterion 3), all six lion / lion-tiger Pokémon orphan from the Tiger row, leaving it entirely empty.

## Design lore

Per Bulbapedia:
- **[Pyroar](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pyroar_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — based on a lion. Fire/Normal. Classified as the Royal Pokémon.
- **[Solgaleo](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Solgaleo_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — based on a lion with influences from both lions and tigers. Steel/Psychic, the Sunne Pokémon.
- **[Entei](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Entei_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — primarily based on a lion with shisa (Okinawan lion-dog) influences. Pure Fire.
- **[Suicune](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Suicune_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — based on the qilin (麒麟) and various large cats. Pure Water. Aurora Pokémon.
- **[Hisuian Arcanine](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Arcanine_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — depicted as a lion-tiger composite in Hisuian form. Fire/Rock.

Note: Suicune's qilin (Chinese unicorn-deer) coding is interesting and complicates the strict orphan — qilin is a canonical Chinese mythological creature. Could argue Suicune passes via qilin-coding rather than lion-tiger-coding. But qilin doesn't fit the Tiger slot either; qilin would be its own thing.

## Market evidence

**Year of the Tiger 2022:** Pokémon GO ran a Lunar New Year event but did *not* explicitly bundle lions for the Tiger Year. The featured Pokémon skewed toward Litten line (Fire Tiger heel-wrestler lore — but Litten is house-cat-scaled at base, orphaned per cat-rule).

The Pokémon TCG China launch in 2022 coincided with Year of the Tiger but featured general-purpose Lunar New Year sets (Pokémon 151 New Year Gift Box) rather than lion-themed specifically. Source: [TCGHobby — Chinese New Year products](https://www.tcghobby.com/products/chinese-new-pokemon-charizard-ex-premium-collection-box-exclusive-taiwan-release).

**Western markets** routinely do bundle lions with tigers as "big cats." Pokémon Center seasonal merchandise in the US has packaged Pyroar / Solgaleo / Entei in tiger-adjacent contexts. But the question is the *Chinese* market specifically.

**Chinese-language community sentiment** (from [Bulbagarden 2008 thread on Chinese influences](https://bulbagarden.net/threads/pokemon-so-chinese-infleunced.29920/) and adjacent fan discussions) supports the strict-character distinction. Chinese fans tend to recognize Pyroar/Solgaleo as 狮, not 虎. Year of the Tiger collectors specifically wanting tiger-themed boxes would feel a lion mismatch — the same way an American buying a "year of the eagle" collection wouldn't accept a hawk.

## Counter-argument: Suicune via qilin

Suicune's actual design references qilin (麒麟), a canonical Chinese mythological creature — auspicious, hooved with a lion-like head, associated with rulers and harvest. Qilin is *not* a tiger, but it IS Chinese cultural-canon. Suicune in Year of the Tiger would feel off, but Suicune in *any* zodiac slot is awkward because qilin doesn't map to a slot.

## Recommendation

**Keep lions orphaned from the Tiger row.** The strict-character distinction is a real cultural fact, not a project artifact. Unlike Bouffalant (where modern Mandarin 北美野牛 explicitly subsumes bison under 牛), there is no Chinese term that subsumes 狮 under 虎. The animals are linguistically and culturally separate, and the market reflects that.

Tiger row stays fully open. The page's "Findings" section already calls this out as the most informative pattern in the grid: Pokémon designed many big cats, none of them tigers.

**Possible exception worth considering:** Litten line (Fire Tiger heel-wrestler explicit in Incineroar's lore). Litten is a house cat at base form (orphaned via Great Race myth), but Incineroar is specifically marketed as a tiger / wrestler. If we apply the "line-as-unit via the endpoint" precedent (Mamoswine via Swinub, Mudbray-Mudsdale via Mudsdale), the Litten line could fit Fire Tiger via Incineroar's tiger endpoint coding. Worth a separate case file.

**Status:** Tiger row stays fully empty under the China-max ruleset. Litten line is a reasonable separate audit (see future case 09).
