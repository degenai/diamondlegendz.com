# Case 05 — Houndoom: Fire Goat or Fire Dog?

**Question:** Houndoom has both ram-horns (decorative) and a hellhound silhouette. Which row does it anchor — Fire Goat (via horns), Fire Dog (via canine body), or stays orphaned (via European Christian / Baphomet symbolism)?

## Design lore

Per [Bulbapedia: Houndoom](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Houndoom_(Pok%C3%A9mon)): Dark/Fire, Dark Pokémon. "Houndoom is a quadrupedal, demonic dog Pokémon. It has an orange torso, a black back, and head with a long, pointed snout." Origins listed: "based on demonic black dogs (such as Black Shuck), barghests, hellhounds, and similar canids of European folklore. Its horns and arrow-tipped tail are reminiscent of demonic imagery."

So Houndoom is unambiguously **canine-bodied** (quadrupedal, snout, dog-coded silhouette) with **decorative goat-horns** added for demonic European symbolism. It's a fire dog *with horns*, not a horned ruminant.

## Strict-rule verdicts

**Earlier ruling (orphaned both):** Houndoom orphans from Goat row because decorative horns aren't pastoral-ruminant horns; orphans from Dog row because hellhound is European Christian symbolism, not Chinese 狗. Both orphans were defensible but conservative.

**Under criterion 8 (Goat):** Goat-coded requires "horned ruminant: goat, sheep, or ram silhouette interchangeable. The character doesn't distinguish among them." Houndoom is *not* a ruminant — it's a predatory canid with horns. The horns are accessories, not anatomy that makes the animal a 羊. Houndoom should NOT be in Goat row.

**Under criterion 11 (Dog):** Domestic dogs only. Houndoom is hellhound-coded, demon-tier rather than companion. Strict reading orphans it.

## Market evidence

Houndoom isn't a tier-one TPC zodiac mascot — no Year-of-Dog event highlighted Houndoom specifically that I can document. But Houndoom IS one of the most popular Dark-type dogs in collector circles, and Pokémon Center has shipped Houndoom merchandise consistently.

The 2018 Year-of-the-Dog Pokémon GO event featured Poochyena, Growlithe, Eevee, Snubbull, Electrike — but did not specifically feature Houndoom (which already had widespread collectible distribution, didn't need event boost). Source: [Pokémon Blog — Year of the Dog 2018](https://pokemonblog.com/2018/02/15/you-can-now-celebrate-the-year-of-the-dog-in-pokemon-go-during-the-lunar-new-year-event-featuring-shiny-poochyena-poochyena-growlithe-eevee-snubbull-and-electrike-with-bonus-stardust/).

In Chinese collector forums, Houndoom is broadly accepted as a "fire dog" (火狗) — the hellhound coding doesn't disqualify it, since Chinese collectors treat the design as canine-with-fire-and-horns. The horns are read as design flourish, not as goat-coding.

## Element fit

Houndoom is Dark/Fire. Fire is on the card cleanly — no R5 stretching needed. Fire Dog placement passes element-honest immediately.

The Dark-half is the only structural concern. Per the existing "Poison + terrestrial habitat → Earth" R5 extension, "Dark + canine-coded" could analogously extend, but for Fire Dog placement the Fire typing alone is enough.

## Recommendation

**Place Houndoom in Fire Dog (depth 1, anchor Houndoom — line: Houndour-Houndoom).** Rationale:

1. Visually a dog with horns, not a ruminant with canine features — fails criterion 8 (Goat) cleanly.
2. Fire typing on the card lands the element via R5 directly — no extension needed.
3. The "hellhound is European symbolism" was the original orphan reason for Dog row, but that's a Tier 3 cultural-memory exclusion and the market-acceptance test (Case 04) overrides it. Chinese collectors recognize Houndoom as a fire dog regardless of European folklore origin.
4. The decorative horns don't change the silhouette enough to flip it into Goat row.

This **also fills Fire Dog** (currently anchored on Arcanine + Hisuian Arcanine, depth 3) — adding Houndour-Houndoom would push Fire Dog to 3 lines = depth 4.

**Status:** Recommended placement — Houndoom in Fire Dog. Note in Fire Dog cluster description: "Houndour-Houndoom (Dark/Fire — quadrupedal canine with decorative horns; market accepts as 火狗 fire-dog despite European hellhound lore)."
