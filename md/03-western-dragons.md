# Case 03 — Western dragons (Charizard, Reshiram, Tyranitar) → Dragon row

**Question:** Should Western-heraldic dragons (Charizard, Reshiram, Turtonator, Volcarona) and kaiju (Tyranitar line) be restored to the Dragon row under the market-acceptance test, or do they orphan via criterion 5 strict (Chinese loong + carp-dragon only)?

## The structural problem

龙 (lóng) in Chinese culture is the loong specifically — serpentine, scaled, whiskered, four-clawed, sky/water-associated. Chinese mythology distinguishes the loong sharply from Western heraldic dragons (winged fire-breathers) and from Japanese kaiju tradition.

Per criterion 5 strict: Charizard line (winged fire-breather), Reshiram (Vast White Pokémon, white-feathered), Turtonator (turtle-dragon), Volcarona (fire-bug), Tyranitar / Larvitar line (kaiju armored saurian) all orphan from the Dragon row. Same for most pseudo-legendary Dragon-types (Salamence, Garchomp, Hydreigon, Goodra, Kommo-o, Dragapult, Baxcalibur). The row collapses to two filled cells (Earth Dragon: Drampa; Water Dragon: Kingdra/Palkia/Gyarados).

## Design lore

Per Bulbapedia:
- **[Charizard](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Charizard_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — Fire/Flying, classified Flame Pokémon. Mega Charizard X is Fire/Dragon. Western heraldic dragon iconography (winged fire-breather, Christian-era European symbolism per [Marina Garrow](https://marinagarrow.home.blog/2019/07/15/gyarados-the-chinese-dragon/)).
- **[Reshiram](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Reshiram_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — Dragon/Fire, Vast White Pokémon. Tao trio member with Zekrom and Kyurem. Visually feathered, more bird-dragon than loong.
- **[Tyranitar](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Tyranitar_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — Rock/Dark, Armor Pokémon. Explicitly kaiju-coded; designer commentary cites Godzilla.
- **[Drampa](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Drampa_(Pok%C3%A9mon))** — Normal/Dragon, Placid Pokémon. Cited inspiration: the Chinese Zhulong (燭龍, the torch-dragon).

## Market evidence — empirically devastating to the strict rule

**This is the case where the market test most clearly overrides the strict-character ruling.**

The Pokémon Company has *repeatedly* shipped Charizard-themed product to Chinese-speaking markets for Year of the Dragon:

- **2024 Pokémon TCG Chinese New Year Charizard EX Premium Collection Box** — exclusive to Taiwan and Hong Kong, released February 2, 2024. Source: [TCGHobby](https://www.tcghobby.com/products/chinese-new-pokemon-charizard-ex-premium-collection-box-exclusive-taiwan-release).
- **2024 Year of the Dragon Charizard collection** with Pokémon TCG release. Source: [The Poke Court — Year of the Dragon 2024 collection](https://thepokecourt.com/collections/year-of-the-dragon-lunar-new-year-2024).
- **eBay listings** confirm widespread Asian-market secondary trading of Year of the Dragon Charizard product. Source: [eBay listing — 2024 Pokémon TCG Chinese New Year Charizard Exclusive Collection](https://www.ebay.com/itm/235063794222).

The Pokémon Company explicitly chose Charizard as their Year of the Dragon flagship for the Chinese-speaking market. They did not choose Drampa, Kingdra, or any loong-coded design. The market spoke through the publisher: Charizard *is* the Dragon-row anchor for collector purposes, regardless of cultural-design history.

Similarly, **the 2026 Dragon Boat Festival collection** (Chinese mainland release) features Pikachu / Magikarp / Gyarados / Bulbasaur / Chikorita per Pokémon China — leaning into the carp-dragon transformation cleanly. So TPC *does* sometimes lean culturally Chinese for Dragon themes; they don't always go Western.

## The conflict

We have two competing facts:

1. **Strict cultural-design history:** Charizard is Western-heraldic, not loong. The grid's scarcity-of-loong scholarship is real and informative.
2. **Empirical market behavior:** TPC ships Charizard for Chinese Year of the Dragon. Collectors building Year-of-Dragon boxes will absolutely include Charizard.

Both facts are true. The question is whether the grid serves design-history scholarship or collector market guidance.

## Recommendation

**Restore Western dragons to the Dragon row under a clearly articulated market-test exception.** Specifically:

- **Fire Dragon:** Charizard line, Reshiram, Turtonator. Ship with note "TPC has marketed Charizard collections in Taiwan/Hong Kong for Year of the Dragon — empirical market acceptance."
- **Earth Dragon:** Garchomp line, Flygon line, Zygarde, Tyranitar (kaiju as earth-dragon flex). Drampa already there.
- **Metal Dragon:** Dialga, Duraludon-Archaludon, Hisuian Goodra (Steel/Dragon).
- **Water Dragon:** existing (Kingdra, Palkia, Gyarados).
- **Wood Dragon:** Sceptile (Mega is Grass/Dragon), Flapple, Appletun, Tropius.

**The Findings section** keeps the cultural-design observation about Western-vs-loong dominance as the project's scholarly nuance. The grid is now both honest about the dex's Western-leaning Dragon roster AND useful for collectors who want to build Year-of-Dragon boxes that the Chinese market has already validated.

The two findings coexist: the dex over-indexes Western dragons (true), AND the Chinese market accepts those Western dragons for Year of the Dragon collectibles (also true). The grid doesn't have to pretend the dex is loong-rich; it just has to acknowledge that for collection-building purposes, the typed Dragon-roster fills the cells the market expects.

**Status:** Recommended restoration pending discussion. If approved: Dragon row goes from 2 filled cells to 5 filled cells, with the Findings note explicitly preserving the loong-scarcity scholarship as a cultural-design observation about the dex *itself*, separate from its market-merchandised use.
